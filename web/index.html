The problem

White-light scanning muddies color. Pro workflows are a mess.

Dye crosstalk from broadband illumination

When you backlight a negative with a white diffuser, each film dye layer absorbs across all three color channels simultaneously. The result is color contamination — muddy skintones, shifted neutrals, wasted dynamic range from the outset.

A terminal + three tools + guesswork

The prior art: a Python script, a browser-based Sony remote, a flat-field correction spreadsheet, and a manual compositing step — all stitched together with notes-app procedures and human error. Drop one flag and your roll is contaminated.

How it works

A deterministic pipeline.
No steps skipped. No guesses made.

01
Narrowband triplet capture

Scanlight fires your Scanlight v4 LED panel in sequence — red only, green only, blue only — triggering the Sony a7CR for each. Three RAW frames per film image, each channel clean and unmixed. A configurable settle time ensures the sensor and illumination are stable before each shutter.

02
Per-channel flat-field correction

Before your scan session, Calibrate captures a blank frame under each LED. The resulting correction mask compensates for illumination falloff corner-to-corner, independently per channel. Each captured RAW is divided by its channel's FFC mask — the light is made perfectly even before compositing begins.

03
Background composite to Linear DNG

The moment a triplet is complete, compositing begins in the background — while you advance the film to the next frame. The three corrected channels are merged and written as a Linear DNG with embedded camera color profiles. The composite pipeline streams continuously; by the time you scan the last frame, your roll is already done.

04
Invert in Negative Lab Pro or Lightroom

Linear DNG files drop directly into your existing negative inversion workflow. Embedded ICC profiles ensure your color space is correctly declared. Scanlight's job ends here — clean input in, clean result out.

The app

Four tabs. One source of truth for your rig.

LIGHT
Direct LED panel control

Individual R/G/B sliders and on/off buttons give you live control of the Scanlight v4 LED unit. Use it to inspect negatives by channel or set custom balance for unusual film stocks.

SETTINGS
Every capture parameter, validated live

Roll name, output folder, trigger mode, per-channel LED levels, settle time, FFC calibration path, camera model, composite format — all in one panel with inline validation. No more scripts to edit.

CALIBRATE
One-button FFC with verdict readout

Capture flat-field references per channel. Scanlight measures illumination falloff % and uniformity %, shows a CLEAN / ACCEPTABLE / FAIL verdict per channel, then wires the calibration directly into your scan session.

SCAN
Capture loop with streaming status

Start/Stop, Capture Frame, Retake, live frame counter. Each frame moves through captured → compositing → done as composites stream in the background — no end-of-roll wait.

Capabilities

Built for the serious archivist.

Narrowband channel separation

Narrow-peak R/G/B LEDs illuminate each film dye layer independently, eliminating the crosstalk that makes white-light scans difficult to invert cleanly.

Streaming background compositing

Each triplet composites the moment it's captured — in the background, while you advance to the next frame. No batch wait at the end of your roll.

Per-channel flat-field correction

Calibrate independently measures falloff and uniformity for R, G, and B. Each channel's correction mask is applied before compositing — even light, edge to edge.

Native macOS application

Not a web app, not a Python script. A proper macOS app with SwiftUI controls, hardware-safe state management, and zero terminal setup required.

Linear DNG with embedded profiles

Output is a standard Linear DNG with embedded camera color profiles — ready for Negative Lab Pro, Lightroom, or any raw processor that understands linear input.

Hardware-safe state machine

An internal state machine guarantees the camera and LED panel are never claimed by two processes simultaneously. No race conditions. No corrupted frames.
